Building a GCP-Based GIS Ecosystem for the Colombian Amazon

Date Published: May 18, 2024

Introduction

The integration of advanced cloud technologies and geographic information systems (GIS) offers transformative potential for remote and underserved communities. This newsletter delves into the creation of a GIS ecosystem using Google Cloud Platform (GCP) tools, aimed at enhancing communication and data management for the Colombian Amazon region.

Main Content

Our project leverages a variety of GCP tools such as BigQuery, GCP SQL, GCP Functions, Compute, and IAM, alongside Google Earth Engine, to develop a comprehensive GIS ecosystem. The goal is to distribute data efficiently through APIs and services, ensuring accessibility for stakeholders in both remote Amazonian areas and urban centers.

Current Progress

We are currently focused on creating dashboards and indicators, using these tools to visualize and analyze data. The next phase will involve implementing spatial solutions to transform our web system into a fully-fledged GIS ecosystem.

Challenges and Solutions

One of the main challenges is ensuring the system functions effectively in areas with low latency and limited bandwidth, such as the Colombian Amazon. By utilizing Starlink's satellite internet and wrapping our REST API with a GraphQL API, we aim to optimize data transfer and enhance communication efficiency.

Future Plans

Next week, we will start integrating spatial solutions to support GIS functionalities. Our vision includes creating a communication bridge between the Amazon region, the national government, and third-party enterprises, aligning with the objectives of Gaia Amazonas.

Key Points

  • Utilization of GCP tools for developing a GIS ecosystem.
  • Focus on optimizing data distribution and communication.
  • Challenges of low latency and bandwidth addressed using Starlink and GraphQL API.
  • Future plans include integrating spatial solutions and enhancing GIS functionalities.
